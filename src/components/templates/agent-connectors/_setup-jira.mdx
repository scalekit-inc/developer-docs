Register your Scalekit environment with the Jira connector so Scalekit handles the authentication flow and token lifecycle for you. The connection name you create will be used to identify and invoke the connection programmatically. You'll need your app credentials from the [Atlassian Developer Console](https://developer.atlassian.com/apps/).

<Steps>
1. ### Set up auth redirects

    - In [Scalekit dashboard](https://app.scalekit.com), go to **Agent Auth** → **Create Connection**.

    - Find **Jira** from the list of providers and click **Create**.

    - Copy the redirect URI. It looks like `https://<SCALEKIT_ENVIRONMENT_URL>/sso/v1/oauth/<CONNECTION_ID>/callback`.

      <img src={useOwnCredentialsImg.src} alt="Configure Jira Connection with redirect URI" width="540" />

    - In the [Atlassian Developer Console](https://developer.atlassian.com/apps/), open your app and go to **APIs and Features** → **OAuth 2.0 (3LO)**.

    - Paste the copied URI into the **Callback URL** field and save.

      <img src={addRedirectUriImg.src} alt="Adding callback URL in Atlassian Developer Console for Jira" width="540" />

2. ### Get client credentials

    In the [Atlassian Developer Console](https://developer.atlassian.com/apps/), open your app and go to **App Details**:

    - **Client ID** — listed under **Client ID**
    - **Client Secret** — listed under **Secret**

3. ### Add credentials in Scalekit

    - In [Scalekit dashboard](https://app.scalekit.com), go to **Agent Auth** → **Connections** and open the connection you created.

    - Enter your credentials:
      - **Client ID** (from your Atlassian app)
      - **Client Secret** (from your Atlassian app)
      - **Permissions** — select the Jira API scopes your app needs (see [Jira OAuth scopes](https://developer.atlassian.com/cloud/jira/platform/scopes-for-oauth-2-3LO-and-forge-apps/))

      <img src={addCredentialsImg.src} alt="Adding credentials for Jira in Scalekit dashboard" width="540" />
    - Click **Save**.
</Steps>
