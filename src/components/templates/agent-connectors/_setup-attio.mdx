Register your Scalekit environment with the Attio connector so Scalekit handles the OAuth flow and token lifecycle for you. The connection name you create will be used to identify and invoke the connection programmatically.

<Steps>
1. ### Copy the redirect URI from Scalekit

   - In [Scalekit dashboard](https://app.scalekit.com), go to **Agent Auth** → **Create Connection**. Find **Attio** and click **Create**. Copy the **Redirect URI** — it looks like:
     `https://<SCALEKIT_ENVIRONMENT_URL>/sso/v1/oauth/<CONNECTION_ID>/callback`

      ![Copy redirect URI from Scalekit dashboard](@/assets/docs/agent-connectors/attio/use-own-credentials-redirect-uri.png)

   Keep this tab open — you'll return to it in step 3.

2. ### Register the redirect URI in your Attio OAuth app

   - Go to [build.attio.com](https://build.attio.com), open your app, and click the **OAuth** tab.

   - Under **Redirect URIs**, click **+ New redirect URI** and paste the redirect URI you copied from Scalekit.

   - Copy the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** shown on this page — you will need them in the next step.

      ![Attio OAuth app showing Client ID, Client Secret, and Redirect URIs](@/assets/docs/agent-connectors/attio/add-redirect-uri.png)

3. ### Add credentials in Scalekit

   - Return to [Scalekit dashboard](https://app.scalekit.com) → **Agent Auth** → **Connections** and open the connection you created in step 1.

   - Enter the following:
     - **Client ID** — from your Attio OAuth app
     - **Client Secret** — from your Attio OAuth app
     - **Permissions** (scopes — see [Attio OAuth scopes reference](https://developers.attio.com/docs/authentication))

      ![Add credentials in Scalekit dashboard](@/assets/docs/agent-connectors/attio/add-credentials.png)

   - Click **Save**.
</Steps>
