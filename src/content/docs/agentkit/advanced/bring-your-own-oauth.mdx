By default, Scalekit uses its own OAuth app credentials when your users go through the OAuth consent flow. This works for development and testing, but in production your users will see Scalekit's name and branding on the consent screen — not yours.

**Bring your own credentials** lets you replace Scalekit's shared OAuth credentials with your own. Once configured, users see your app name, logo, and terms on every OAuth consent screen.

## What changes when you use your own credentials

- **Consent screens** display your application's name and branding
- **Rate limits and quotas** are tied to your OAuth app, not Scalekit's shared pool
- **Provider relationship** is direct — your OAuth app appears in provider dashboards and audit logs
- **Compliance** — useful if your organization requires a direct relationship with each OAuth provider

Nothing changes in your code or the Scalekit SDK. The switch is purely a dashboard configuration on the connection.

## Configure your credentials

<Steps>
1. ### Create an OAuth app with the provider

   In the provider's developer console, create a new OAuth app (or use an existing one). You'll need the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** it generates.

2. ### Open the connection in Scalekit Dashboard

   Go to **Agent Auth > Connections** and click **Edit** on the connection you want to update.

3. ### Switch to your own credentials

   Select **Use your own credentials**. The form expands to show Client ID and Client Secret fields.

4. ### Copy the redirect URI

   Scalekit displays a **Redirect URI** for this connection. Copy it.

5. ### Add the redirect URI to your OAuth app

   In the provider's console, add Scalekit's Redirect URI to the list of authorized redirect URIs for your OAuth app.

   <Aside type="caution" title="Redirect URI must match exactly">
     The URI must match character-for-character. A mismatch will cause OAuth flows to fail with a redirect_uri_mismatch error.
   </Aside>

6. ### Enter your credentials and save

   Enter the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** from your OAuth app and click **Save**.

   All new OAuth flows for this connection will now use your credentials.

</Steps>

## Existing connected accounts

Switching credentials does not affect connected accounts that are already active. However, if a user re-authorizes or connects for the first time after the switch, they will go through the consent screen with your new credentials.

If you need all users to see your branding immediately, generate new authorization links and prompt them to re-authorize.
