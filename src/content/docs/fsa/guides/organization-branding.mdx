When a user from Acme Corp signs in, they see Acme Corp's logo instead of your platform's logo. Organization branding replaces the workspace logo with each customer's own logo across three surfaces, without changing colors, layout, or any other workspace setting.

| Surface | What changes |
|---|---|
| **Hosted login page** | Replaces the workspace logo and updates the heading to "Login to \{org name\}". The sign-up page always shows your workspace logo. |
| **Admin portal** | Replaces the logo in the portal header. The title remains "Admin Portal". |
| **End-user app** | Replaces the logo in the top navigation header. |

## Enable the feature

Toggle **Organization branding** on in **Customization > Branding** in your Scalekit dashboard.

![Scalekit Branding settings page with the Organization Branding toggle](@/assets/docs/fsa/organizations/organization_branding_configuration.png)

Once enabled, any organization with a `logo_url` shows its own logo on the surfaces above. Organizations without one continue to show your workspace logo — there is no broken image state.

<Aside type="note">
The flag must be enabled at the environment level. Setting `logo_url` on an organization has no effect unless the environment-level flag is on.
</Aside>

## Set an organization's logo

The `logo_url` must point to a publicly accessible image. Scalekit does not host or proxy logo images. To remove a logo, set it to an empty string; the surface falls back to your workspace logo.

### From the dashboard

Open an organization, click **Edit**, and fill in the **Logo URL** field.

![Edit Organization dialog showing the Slug and Logo URL fields](@/assets/docs/fsa/organizations/update-organization-slug-logo.png)

## Relationship to workspace branding

Workspace branding (configured in **Customization > Branding**) sets your platform's default logo, colors, and layout. Organization branding overrides the logo only. Colors, border radius, and layout remain unchanged from your workspace settings.
